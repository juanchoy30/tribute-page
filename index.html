Francisco Herrera Luque

Decoding the history of Venezuela

(Caracas, December 14th 1927 - Caracas, April 15th 1991)

Francisco Herrera Luque (psychiatrist, writer and diplomat) was one of the most important writers of Venezuela. Most of his novels were about the complex history and culture of this country. His masterpiece is “Los amos del valle”, a historical novel that covers the life in Venezuela, during the colonial times, since the foundation of Caracas to the birth of Simón Bolívar.

Time line

  • 1927 - Born in Caracas, Venezuela.
  • 1952 - Graduated as a medical doctor from "Universidad de Salamanca"
  • 1956 - Married with María Margarita Terán Austria
  • 1961 - Obtained his psychiatrist PhD and made his thesis "Los Viajeros de Indias"
  • 1970´s - Founded the course of psychiatry in "Universidad Central de Venezuela" and became a full professor.
  • Mid 1970´s- Was appointed ambassador of Venezuela in Mexico.
  • 1991 - Died from a heart atrack in Caracas, Venezuela.
